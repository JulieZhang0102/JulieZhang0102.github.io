starBook Web APP Development

Basic Information

Role: Product Manager and Web APP Developer
Industry: Non-Profit Organization
Tech Tool: Python Django
Project Length: 3-month development & up to date maintenance
Client: International Chinese Reading Association (ICRA)
Link: starBook Web APP Link

Project Story

Client Demands Target Users Wireframe Development Launch Expansion

Understanding Client’s Demands and Requirements

Besides carefully listening to my client, I worked with my client to envision the product from random ideas to a coherent application program with specific goals.

Main Challenges:

      1. The client did not have a clear vision for the project.
      2. The client was not familiar with Web APP development.

Strategy:

      1. Through conversations with different stakeholders, I discussed their needs, sorted out their ideas, and transformed them into effective product goals.
      2. I researched similar products or prototypes to visualize thoughts and clarify any confusion.
      3. The key points were to create flexibility for stakeholders to think and propose new thoughts.

Deliverable: A project proposal details the project vision, project goal, implementation plan, potential risks, and communication plan.

Target Users’ Analysis and Web APP Flowchart Design

The Web APP is designed for two types of users, program participating parents and program managers. Two basic personas are created below.

Lucy, 35 years old, a mother of two kids whose ages are 3 and 5. She wants to borrow or buy some Chinese children’s books for her kids but does not know which libraries to go to or what books are recommended. She participates in the starBook program and wants to get some related book information on the program Web APP. At the same time, she could also recommend any book to the program and comment on existing books.

Tim, 28 years old, an ICRA volunteer for the starBook program. He needs to use the program Web APP to manage the book collection list. He has his administration account and can upload, download, modify, and delete any book record in the administration interface.

Deliverable: A flowchart maps the book recommendation or comment process.

Web APP Wireframe Design

To better visualize the Web APP and discuss the design with my client, I sketched main page wireframes and revised them during meetings.

Strategy: I sketched wireframes for my client to make instant changes during our conversations. Thus, we would not waste time making changes to fancy mockup tools.

Deliverable: Final sketch wireframes illustrate structures of the program home page and book collection page.

Web APP Development and Weekly Iteration

I applied the Agile methodology to better interact with my client and improve the project flexibility to changes.

Strategy:

      1. Through iteration meetings, I updated development progress, received feedback from my client, and made improvements spontaneously in a weekly cycle.
      2. I frequently revisited the product goals with my client to make sure the project is on the right track fulfilling the product’s business value.

Significant Improvement: Incorporated batch data import feature simplifies the data entry process for program managers.

Web APP Testing and First Launch

Both internal testing and external feedback collection were implemented.

Internal Testing: The Web APP was firstly tested by program managers to test functionalities and fix bugs.

External Feedback Collection: The Web APP was then launched to collect user experience feedback from participating parents (public users).

Significant Improvement: Integrated Google Books API searches and auto-fills book information for parents, which reduces parents’ workload and encourages them to recommend books through Web APP.

Web APP Maintenance and Expansion

As more feedback is collected, maintenance is scheduled every two months.

Thanks to the positive reaction from users, my client decided to expand the Web APP to be its organization website. I will be responsible for designing more features to continue providing valuable information to the target audience.

Reflection

In reality, clients do not come with well-structured demands. It is critical for me, as a product manager, to help my client envision the product through conversations and prototypes.

Mindpeace Mobile APP Design

Basic Information

Role: Product Manager and Designer
Industry: Mental Health
Tech Tool: Balsamiq
Project Length: 1.5-month school project

Project Story

Idea & Market Users & Pitch Viability & Feasibility UI & UX Development Marketing & Growth

The Big Idea & The Market

Market Opportunity:

There are about 44 million people who struggle with mental illness every year in the US. That is, 1 in 6 people is having different levels of mental illness issues.

---- National Institute of Mental Health


Problems:

  • No public place to express thoughts and release stress
  • No easy way to consistently access stress-relief resources
  • No time/too anxious to meet professional therapist face to face

Mindpeace Solution:

A mobile APP allows people to relieve their stress by sharing thoughts and express concerns. The app will provide stress management resources and create a secure & private environment for people to mitigate anxiety.

Service:

Anonymous public forums

Stress management resources

Professional therapist assistance

Stress relief events

Target Users & Concept Pitch

Target Users:

16 to 30-year-old students and young adults with stressful schedules

Sample Persona:

Name: Max
Age: 24

  • Max is a recent college graduate working an entry-level accounting job in a huge company.
  • Working seems to be his whole life, and even worse he is single.
  • He has few friends and a very minuscule and uninteresting social life.
  • This routine has impacted his mental health very negatively. He is not motivated to do anything and cannot stay concentrated.

Journey Map:

Concept Pitch:

Five concept pitches are conducted with randomly selected target users.

Feedback & Improvements:

  • People thought the APP was a convenient platform for stress relief.
  • The APP should provide more varieties of stress management resources.
  • The APP should organize special stress relief events.

Viability & Feasibility

Competitor Research:

Revenue Model:

  • Advertisements
  • Professional Meditation Guide
  • One-on-one consultation service

Potential Regulatory Constraints:

  • Forum and one-on-one chat information must be secured on the server and keep private.
  • Mental health professionals must be qualified to provide consulting services according to state regulations.

UI & UX

Balsamiq Mockup:

Click Me to Play!

Major UX Improvement:

Original Menu Design: Unless the menu icon is clicked, the menu bar would be hidden off the screen.

Feedback: The first round of testers reported that such a design made it hard to switch between different views.

Improvement: Four view tabs for home, forum, recreation, and therapy are added at the bottom of the screen for the convenient view switch.

Development Strategies

Architecture: Cross-Platform

  • Web: Provide APP marketing information, APP download links, and stress management packages such as Yoga video and meditation guide.
  • Mobile APP: Access to the anonymous public forum, nearby events, live streaming stress relief resources, and one-on-one consultation service.

Operating System and Store: Considering the development cost, the APP will only be available on the Apple Store (iOS) at the first stage.

Development Plan:

Marketing Strategies & Growth Projection

Marketing Strategies:

  • App-Store Optimization: Select strategic key-words for search engine optimization
  • Reviewing Rewards: Offer discounted price services for users who review the APP
  • App Referrals: Design rewards program for referrals that lead to downloads
  • Blogs: Create an alternative medium that attracts followers to the APP
  • Social Media Campaigns: Partner with popular social media influencers in the target market to promote the APP

Growth Projection:

Projected Market Size

Projected Income Statement

Reflection

End users must be engaged throughout the design and development phases. Usability testing is vital for receiving instant feedback and improving user experience.

Lean Operations Consulting

Basic Information

Role: Lean Operations Consultant
Industry: Aerospace
Project Length: 2.5 months
Client: A top aerospace manufacturer in India

Project Story

Problem Targets Root Causes Countermeasures Implementation Results & Processes

Clarify and Break Down the Problem

The Problem:

The lack of operations data & information makes the director disconnected from the company, which leads to other manufacturing issues.

The goal is to bridge the gap so that upper-managers could manage the company with real-time information.

Break Down the Problem:

Target Setting

  • Check 100% of employees’ daily attendance
  • Track 98% of machine downtime & corresponding reasons
  • Digitize and manage 75% of data in real-time
  • Generate reports in 2-3hrs upon request
  • Ensure 90% of the data accuracy using the self-check procedure

Root Cause Analysis

Narrowed-Down Root Causes (red circles in the chart below):

  • For managers: there is a lack of data tracking and management system.
  • For machine operators: the data entry process is too complicated.

Significant Breakthrough:

At first, managers kept telling us there was no data and report available for him to review, so we designed customized sheets to track data and generate reports.

However, we were still getting pushback from machine operators who were responsible for data entry.

After interviewing machine operators and conducting anomalous surveys, we realized that there was only one computer on the whole manufacturing floor. Thus, whenever operators need to input data, they need to walk across the entire factory. This complicated process discourages operators from recording valuable data for the upper-management.

Develop Countermeasures

Material and Information Flow Chart (MIFC) was crafted to understand the manufacturing process and support the design of data-tracking sheets.

Implementation, Iteration, and Improvement

Implementation Process:

Implementation Results:

For data-tracking sheets design:

ERP system research:

      1. Five companies (Microsoft Dynamics, Odoo, ERPnext, Epicor, and Syspro: California) were compared.
      2. Odoo was selected as the optimal ERP system for advantages below:
      • Cloud-based, open source ERP solution
        • -- Open source => new apps constantly being developed
      • Blockchain enabled
        • -- Improved tracking and security
      • 4 million users
        • -- Lots of support through forums
      3. Functionality proposal:
      4. Implementation plan:
      • Odoo Gold Partner Implementation team
        • -- Sales contact information is given
      • Office is 200km from Mysore
        • -- On-site implementation request
        -- Remote implementation assistance
      • Professional consulting service for personalized implementation

Monitor Results and Standardize Processes

Results Achieved

Standardize Successful Processes

Reflection

Talk to all stakeholders involved in your project!!! Different stakeholders may have entirely different expectations for the same product. In this project, managers demand detailed data-tracking sheets for production planning purposes. However, machine operators ask for a simple data entry process with less distraction from their manufacturing work.
